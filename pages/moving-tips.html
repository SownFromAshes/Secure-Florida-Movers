Moving Timeline

Follow this expert timeline for a perfectly organized move

8 Weeks Before

Start researching moving companies, get quotes, and book your preferred date. Create a moving binder to keep all documents organized.

6 Weeks Before

Confirm your moving company and date. Start decluttering - donate, sell, or discard items you don't need. Order moving supplies.

4 Weeks Before

Start using up frozen and perishable food. Begin packing non-essential items. Research your new neighborhood and schools.

2 Weeks Before

Confirm details with your moving company. Start change of address process. Pack everything except essentials.

1 Week Before

Pack a suitcase with essentials for the first week. Confirm utility connections at your new home. Clean out safety deposit box.

Moving Day

Be present for the move. Check inventory lists. Keep important documents with you. Take final meter readings.

Expert Packing Tips

Professional packing hacks from experienced movers

Box Packing Strategy

Pack heavy items in small boxes and light items in large boxes. Fill empty spaces with packing paper to prevent shifting.

  • Use book boxes for heavy items
  • Label boxes on multiple sides
  • Pack boxes to 90% capacity
  • Use color-coded labels by room
Pro Tip

Fragile Items Protection

Wrap fragile items individually in bubble wrap or packing paper. Use dishes boxes with dividers for plates and glasses.

  • Wrap each item separately
  • Use towels for extra padding
  • Pack plates vertically like records
  • Mark "FRAGILE" clearly on boxes

Clothing & Linens

Use wardrobe boxes for hanging clothes. Pack out-of-season items first and keep essentials accessible.

  • Use vacuum bags for bulky items
  • Pack shoes in original boxes
  • Use linens as packing material
  • Keep outfit changes accessible

Moving Day Survival Kit

Essential items to keep with you on moving day

Important Documents

Keep all essential paperwork in a clearly marked folder that travels with you, not in the moving truck.

First Aid & Medications

Pack a basic first aid kit and any daily medications in an easily accessible bag for immediate needs.

Food & Water

Pack snacks, water bottles, and paper plates for moving day meals. Consider ordering pizza for the crew.

Basic Tools

Keep a small toolkit with screwdriver, hammer, and measuring tape for immediate furniture assembly.

Change of Clothes

Pack comfortable clothes, work gloves, and a complete outfit for the next day in an overnight bag.

Communication Essentials

Ensure phone chargers, important phone numbers, and contact info for utilities are easily accessible.

Money-Saving Moving Tips

Expert advice to reduce your moving costs

Book During Off-Peak Times

Move during mid-month, mid-week, or fall/winter for significantly lower rates. Avoid summer months and end-of-month dates when possible.

Declutter Before Moving

Sell, donate, or discard items you don't need. Moving fewer items reduces both time and cost. Consider having a garage sale weeks before your move.

Pack Yourself

Handle your own packing to save on labor costs. Focus professional packing services on fragile or valuable items only.

Avoid These Common Moving Mistakes

Learn from others' experiences to ensure a smooth move

1

Not Getting Multiple Quotes

Always get at least 3 quotes from different moving companies. Prices can vary significantly, and you'll want to compare services offered.

2

Forgetting to Label Boxes

Proper labeling saves hours during unpacking. Include room destination and brief contents description on multiple sides of each box.

3

Packing Everything Last Minute

Start packing non-essential items weeks in advance. Last-minute packing leads to poor organization and potential damage.

4

Not Measuring Furniture

Measure large furniture and doorways at your new home. Ensure your big pieces will fit through doors and up stairs.

5

Ignoring Moving Insurance

Understand your coverage options. Basic coverage is minimal - consider additional insurance for valuable items.
