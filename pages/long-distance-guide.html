What is Long-Distance Moving?

Long-distance moving, also called interstate moving, involves relocating your household goods across state lines or over distances greater than 100 miles. Unlike local moves that are charged by the hour, long-distance moves are priced based on the weight of your shipment and the distance traveled.

What to Expect During Your Long-Distance Move

Key differences between local and long-distance moving

Extended Timeline

Long-distance moves take 1-5 business days for delivery, depending on distance. We provide pickup and delivery windows to help you plan.

Weight-Based Pricing

Costs are calculated by the weight of your shipment and distance traveled, not by time. We weigh the truck before and after loading.

Federal Regulations

Interstate moves are regulated by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration. We provide all required documentation and follow strict guidelines.

Understanding Long-Distance Moving Costs

Factors that affect your interstate moving price

Weight of Shipment

The primary factor in long-distance moving costs. We weigh the truck before and after loading to determine exact weight. Average 3-bedroom home weighs 9,000-12,000 lbs.

Distance Traveled

Mileage from origin to destination affects pricing. Cross-country moves cost more than regional moves. We calculate the most efficient route for your move.

Timing & Season

Summer months (May-September) have higher demand and prices. Moving mid-week or mid-month often provides better rates than peak times.

Essential Long-Distance Moving Tips

Expert advice for a successful interstate relocation

Research Your New State

Learn about your new state's taxes, vehicle registration requirements, voting registration, and any professional license transfers you'll need to complete.

Choose Full Value Protection

For long-distance moves, consider upgrading from basic coverage to Full Value Protection. The longer transit time increases the importance of comprehensive insurance.

Keep Essentials With You

Pack a suitcase with 1-2 weeks of essentials since delivery windows can vary. Include medications, important documents, and comfort items.

Update Your Address Early

Submit change of address forms 2-3 weeks before your move. Notify banks, employers, subscription services, and government agencies of your new address.

Research Moving Companies Thoroughly

Verify licensing with FMCSA, read reviews, and get written estimates from multiple companies. Avoid companies that demand large deposits or give estimates without seeing your belongings.

Plan for Storage if Needed

If there's a gap between your move-out and move-in dates, arrange storage in advance. This is common with long-distance moves due to timing coordination.
